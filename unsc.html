What is the UNSC?

It is one of the six principal organs of the United Nations, charged with the responsibility of maintaining international peace and security.

The Committee Agenda

"Discuss The Urgent Humanitarian Situation in The Gaza Strip and the Need For Immediate Ceasefire and Lifting of the Blockade By Israel."

Summary

As the tides of time keep ebbing and flowing the political face of the world keeps on changing. Today the threat of groups using force and terror as their prime weapons is at its peak and as the apex organ of the United Nations, the United Nations Security Council is required to deliberate on the issue with the highest priority.

Centuries-old land rights conflicts in the Palestine/Israel region have fueled numerous disputes and have led to the emergence of various fringe factions. These ongoing historical tensions are a significant concern for the United Nations Security Council (UNSC), as they impact regional stability, security, and efforts to find lasting solutions to the complex issues in the area. The UNSC continues to address these conflicts to promote peace and security in the region.

It will be required of all delegates to look into the matter with new perspectives so as to identify, quantify and then terminate the issue that is prevalent in the Middle Eastern region so as to allow the post crisis solutions to sink in with maximum effect. Since the agenda is vast with many underlying layers of complexity, it is necessary to give due consideration to the status quo regarding policies, beliefs and culture.

Seeking new discretion, debate and discussion, hoping to witness exceptional diplomacy, the UNSC cordially invites you on board.
