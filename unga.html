What is the UNGA?

The United Nations General Assembly (UNGA) is one of the six main organs of the United Nations, serving as a global forum for all member states to convene, discuss, and collaborate on a wide range of international issues. As the world's largest deliberative body, the UNGA provides each member state with an equal voice, making it a cornerstone of multilateral diplomacy

The Committee Agenda

"COMBATTING THE RISE OF AUTHORITARIANISM AND HYPER-NATIONALISM"

Summary

It’s 2023, the Great Powers of the World are back to their Cold-War style aggression. The Powers, thanks to their great thirst for world dominance and hegemony, have found a way to monopolize: by arming their allies. The armed forces of various nations are stationed at different bases around the world, because of the various bilateral Status Of Forces Agreements (SOFA) between various nations. The mere presence of such forces has given rise to antagonism, and created differences not just among some rival bloc members, but sometimes it has severed the ties between the host and the visitors too.

With all the international geopolitics playing its role in this Global Theatre, are there any hideous villains lurking behind the scenes? Shall the world achieve détente? Or has the 'Curtain' fallen on the hopes of achieving international peace and security?
Come find out in the United Nations General Assembly.
