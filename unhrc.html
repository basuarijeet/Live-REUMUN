What is the UNHRC?

The United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC) is a pivotal international body dedicated to the promotion and protection of human rights across the globe. Established in 2006, the UNHRC succeeded the UN Commission on Human Rights and has since become a key player in the UN's efforts to address human rights violations, engage in dialogue with nations, and advocate for the principles of human rights.

The UNHRC is a humanitarian appointed committee that aims to tackle some of the most vital, inhumane, and repulsive global issues.

The Committee Agenda

"PROTECTING VICTIMS OF HUMAN TRAFFICKING IN CONFLICT AND POST-CONFLICT SITUATIONS."

Summary

Civil and Political Rights: These include the right to life, liberty, and security of person, as well as freedoms of expression, assembly, and religion. They also encompass the right to participate in government through free and fair elections and the prohibition of torture, discrimination, and arbitrary detention.

Economic, Social, and Cultural Rights: These encompass the right to work, education, and an adequate standard of living. They include the right to healthcare, housing, and education, aiming to ensure that all individuals can lead a life of dignity.

Collective Rights: Some human rights are applicable to groups, such as the right to self-determination for indigenous peoples or the right to a clean and sustainable environment.

Solidarity Rights: These rights include the right to peace and development, emphasizing the interconnectedness of human rights and the importance of addressing global issues collectively.
